Blinds|Forced bets that start the action. The small blind (half the big blind) and big blind (minimum bet) rotate around the table each hand.
Button|The dealer position marker that rotates clockwise after each hand. The player on the button acts last post-flop.
Hole Cards|The two private cards dealt face-down to each player.
Community Cards|The five cards dealt face-up in the middle of the table that all players can use.
Flop|First three community cards
Turn|Fourth community card
River|Fifth and final community card
Pot|The total amount of chips in the middle that players are competing to win.
Showdown|When remaining players reveal their hole cards after the final betting round to determine the winner.
Wet Board|A flop with many possible draws and connections (e.g., 9♠8♠7♥). Dangerous because many hands can improve.
Dry Board|A flop with few possible draws (e.g., K♣2♦4♥). Safe for strong made hands.
Connected Board|Cards are close in rank, creating straight possibilities.
Suited Board|Multiple cards of the same suit, creating flush possibilities.
Paired Board|Two cards of the same rank on the board.
Made Hand|A hand that doesn't need to improve to win (e.g., top pair, set, straight).
Drawing Hand|A hand that needs specific cards to become strong (e.g., flush draw, straight draw).
Bluff|Betting or raising with a weak hand to make opponents fold better hands.
Semi-Bluff|Betting with a drawing hand that could improve to the best hand.
Value Bet|Betting with a strong hand to get called by worse hands.
Continuation Bet|Betting on the flop after raising pre-flop.
Check-Raise|Checking when it's your turn, then raising after an opponent bets.
Pot Odds|The ratio between the current pot size and the cost of a potential call.
Implied Odds|Potential future bets you might win if you hit your draw.
Fold Equity|The probability that your bet will make an opponent fold.
Position|Where you sit relative to the button. Early position acts first, late position acts last.
Check|Passing the action to the next player without betting (only possible if no one has bet yet).
Call|Matching the current bet amount.
Raise|Increasing the current bet amount.
Re-raise|Raising after someone else has already raised.
Fold|Discarding your hand and sitting out the remainder of the hand.
